Artist's Statement

My goal is to make images which are not necessarily beautiful but rather reflective.

I crave an active and diverse viewing experience.

The mark you see is not a branch - it's a mark, it's a movement, it's an impression, it's a pathway. Except when that same mark is a branch. The mark is a branch and everything else that shape could depict, it's a branch and nothing else those shapes might depict.

The images come instinctually through a practice of replicating East Asian ink painting techniques - a replication of a replication of a replication.

About Scott

I'm an artist working out of Tokyo, Japan. I have a B.A. from Clark University in Studio Art.

My work is heavily influenced and concerned with East Asian brush painting, stemming my time spent living in Japan studying Japanese language, culture, and art.

I use elements and techniques found in traditional brush painting and apply them in a way which is not instantly recognizable in an effort to bring out traditional ink painting's innate contemporary qualities.
